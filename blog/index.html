Thinking about moving your store to Shopify? Good instinct. But the process feels overwhelming, right?

You're worried about:

  • ❌ Losing all your product data
  • ❌ Customers unable to see their order history
  • ❌ Hours of downtime (lost sales!)
  • ❌ Your SEO rankings disappearing
  • ❌ Having to manually recreate everything

Here's the good news: With the right approach, you can migrate in just 2-5 days and avoid all of these problems.

This guide walks you through it step-by-step. No fluff, no jargon. Just actionable steps you can take today.

1: Should You Migrate to Shopify?

Do You Actually Need to Migrate to Shopify?

Before you start, let's confirm Shopify is right for you.

You should migrate if you're currently on:

  • WooCommerce (requires constant maintenance)
  • BigCommerce (too expensive)
  • Magento (too complex)
  • Squarespace, Etsy, 3dcart, PrestaShop
  • An aging custom-built platform
  • Any platform that's holding you back

You DON'T need to migrate if:

  • You're already on Shopify or Shopify Plus ✅
  • Your business is very small (<10 products, <$1k/month revenue)
  • You're happy with your current platform and it's growing

Still not sure? Read our comparison: WooCommerce vs Shopify | BigCommerce vs Shopify

3. Will You Lose SEO Rankings?

Short answer: No, if you do it right. Here's what you need to do:

  • ✅ Set up 301 redirects (old URL → new URL)
  • ✅ Update your sitemap
  • ✅ Notify Google Search Console
  • ✅ Update internal links

With these steps, you preserve 90-95% of your rankings. Rankings may dip for 2-4 weeks, then recover.

Without these steps? Yes, you'll lose rankings. Don't do that.

4. Will Your Customers Lose Access to Their Accounts?

Shopify doesn't import passwords (for security). So yes, customers need to reset their passwords.

How to handle it:

  • Send an email: "Reset your password here: [link]"
  • Make it easy (one-click reset)
  • Some customers will ignore it (that's normal)

For security-conscious merchants: This is actually a feature, not a bug. Password reset ensures old passwords aren't exposed.

5. What If Something Goes Wrong?

Good news: You've backed up your original data, right?

Always follow this rule: Never delete your old store until 30 days after going live.

If something breaks:

  • You can go back to your old store (have a backup)
  • Rerun the migration to fix issues
  • Customers can still access their old accounts

This is why automation tools are worth it—they can re-run the migration without losing data.

4: The Actual Migration Process

How to Migrate to Shopify (The Simple Version)

Okay, here's where it gets real. The actual migration happens in 7 steps.

Step 1: Set Up Your Shopify Store

  1. Go to Shopify.com and click "Start free trial"
  2. Enter your email and create a password
  3. Follow the setup wizard (takes 10 minutes)

You now have an empty Shopify store ✅

Cost: Free for 3 months (then $39-99/month)

Time needed: 15 minutes

Don't worry about: Customization, branding, or settings yet. We're moving fast.

Step 2: Move Your Products

This is the biggest step. Here's how it works:

If using a migration tool (recommended):

  1. Install Shift It Now (or your tool of choice)
  2. Connect your old store (give it access to your product data)
  3. Choose what to import (products, images, descriptions, pricing)
  4. Click "Start migration"
  5. Wait 1-4 hours (depending on product count)
  6. Review the imported products (takes 30 minutes)
  7. Fix any issues (usually minor)

If doing it manually:

  1. Export products from old store as CSV
  2. Reformat the CSV to match Shopify's template
  3. Upload to Shopify admin > Products > Import
  4. Manually verify each product (time-consuming)

Time needed: 2-4 hours with tool | 40-80+ hours manually

Pro tip: Always test with 10 products first. Don't import everything at once.

Start Free Product Migration →

Step 3: Migrate Your Customers & Orders

If you want customers to see their past orders and account history:

  1. Export customers from old store (email, name, address)
  2. Use a migration tool OR
  3. Import via CSV to Shopify admin > Customers > Import
  4. Send customers an email: "Reset your password here"

Important: Passwords don't transfer. Customers must reset them.

Time needed: 1-2 hours

Backlink opportunity: Write a blog post titled "How to Preserve Customer Data During Ecommerce Migration" — agencies and forums will link to this.

Step 4: Set Up Your Domain

This is critical. Your domain is your brand. Do it right.

Option A: Point Domain to Shopify (Easiest)

  1. Go to your domain registrar (GoDaddy, Namecheap, etc.)
  2. Update DNS settings to point to Shopify
  3. In Shopify admin > Settings > Domains > Add existing domain
  4. Follow the instructions
  5. Done (takes 24-48 hours to propagate)

Option B: Transfer Domain to Shopify (Less common)

  1. Shopify can handle your domain directly
  2. Takes 5-7 days to transfer
  3. Less recommended during migration (adds complexity)

Time needed: 15 minutes

Pro tip: Do this step last (right before launch). Once you point your domain, your old store goes offline.

Step 5: Set Up 301 Redirects (For SEO)

This protects your search rankings.

Every old URL needs to redirect to the new URL:

  • Old: yourstore.com/products/blue-shoes
  • New: yourstore.com/products/blue-athletic-shoes
  • Redirect: /products/blue-shoes → /products/blue-athletic-shoes

How to do it:

  1. In Shopify admin > Settings > URL Redirects
  2. Add all your old URLs and where they should redirect
  3. Test 5-10 redirects to make sure they work
  4. Google sees the redirects and preserves your rankings

Time needed: 2-4 hours

Pro tip: Prioritize product pages and popular blog posts. Don't worry about every single URL.

Step 6: Test, Test, Test (Pre-Launch)

Before you go live, test everything:

On your computer:

  • Browse 10 product pages
  • Add something to cart
  • Complete checkout (test payment)
  • Verify order confirmation email

On mobile:

  • Same steps as above
  • Mobile checkout works smoothly
  • Images display correctly

In Shopify admin:

  • Check inventory counts
  • Verify pricing is correct
  • Confirm shipping settings
  • Test customer login/password reset

Time needed: 1-2 hours

Pro tip: Invite 2-3 trusted customers to test before launch. Get real-world feedback.

Step 7: Launch! (Go Live)

The moment of truth. Here's what happens:

48 hours before:

  • Send email to customers: "Heads up, we're moving to a new platform tomorrow!"

Day of launch:

  • Point your domain to Shopify (the switch happens)
  • Monitor the store for 30 minutes (check for errors)
  • Send announcement email: "We've officially moved!"

First 24 hours:

  • Respond to customer support emails quickly
  • Monitor for broken links or 404 errors
  • Check Google Search Console for crawl errors
  • Be available (in case something breaks)

Time needed: 30 minutes of active work | then monitor for 24 hours

5: SEO Preservation (Critical!)

How to Keep Your SEO Rankings After Migration

This is where most merchants mess up. Don't be that person.

Before Migration: SEO Audit

Document your current rankings:

  1. Go to Google Search Console
  2. Go to Performance report
  3. Take a screenshot of your top 20 keywords
  4. Note which products/pages rank high
  5. Save this somewhere (you'll compare it later)

Why? So you can verify your rankings recover after migration.

During Migration: Set Up Redirects

This is non-negotiable for SEO.

Every old URL must redirect to a new URL. Google sees the redirect and passes ranking authority from the old page to the new page.

Without redirects? Your rankings tank.

How to prioritize redirects:

  • Redirect ALL product pages (these are usually your money pages)
  • Redirect important blog posts
  • Redirect category/collection pages
  • Redirect less important pages (if there's time)

Time needed: 2-4 hours

After Migration: Monitor & Fix

Days 1-3:

  • Check Google Search Console
  • Look for crawl errors (fix any 404s)
  • Check that old URLs redirect to new ones

Week 1-2:

  • Monitor rankings in Google Search Console
  • Expect a temporary dip (2-4 weeks is normal)
  • Rankings should start recovering

Month 1:

  • Check rankings for your top 20 keywords
  • Compare to the screenshot you took before migration
  • If rankings haven't recovered, investigate why

Pro tip: Most rankings recover in 2-4 weeks if you set up redirects correctly.

7: Mistakes to Avoid (Learn From Others)

The 5 Biggest Migration Mistakes (And How to Avoid Them)

Mistake #1: Not Backing Up Your Original Data

What happens: You delete your old store after migration. Then something breaks. You panic.

How to avoid it:

  • Export everything from your old store before migration (products, customers, orders, emails)
  • Keep backups in two places (external drive + cloud)
  • Wait 30-60 days before deleting the old store
  • Keep the export files forever (you never know when you'll need them)

Mistake #2: Rushing the Process

What happens: You migrate on a Friday afternoon without testing. Something breaks. No support available. You lose a weekend of sales.

How to avoid it:

  • Plan 2-4 weeks ahead
  • Migrate on a Tuesday-Thursday
  • Test thoroughly before launch
  • Have 24 hours available for monitoring

Mistake #3: Forgetting 301 Redirects

What happens: You lose all your SEO rankings. Traffic disappears. Revenue drops.

How to avoid it:

  • Set up redirects BEFORE pointing your domain
  • Test at least 10 redirects manually
  • Monitor Google Search Console after launch
  • If you see 404s, fix them immediately

Mistake #4: Not Communicating With Customers

What happens: Customers show up and the store looks different. They get confused. Support tickets flood in.

How to avoid it:

  • Email customers 1 week before: "We're moving to a new platform"
  • Email customers day of: "Here's what changed"
  • Email customers day 1 after: "We're here if you have questions"
  • Pin an announcement on your homepage
  • Be responsive to support emails

Mistake #5: Not Testing the Payment System

What happens: You go live. Customers try to buy. Payment processing is broken. You lose sales.

How to avoid it:

  • Test a real transaction in staging first (use a test credit card)
  • Verify payment gateway is connected
  • Check that order confirmation emails send
  • Monitor payments for the first 24 hours
